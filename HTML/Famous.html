Heron of Alexandria

Heron of Alexandria was a renowned Greek mathematician and engineer who lived during the Greco-Roman period. Known for his inventive spirit, Heron is credited with creating the world's first steam engine, known as the *aeolipile*, and devising numerous other mechanical innovations that demonstrate his deep understanding of geometry and physics.
