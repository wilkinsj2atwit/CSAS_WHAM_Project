Ancient Greek Buildings

Temple of Artemis (Roman Reconstruction)

The Temple of Artemis was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Its Roman reconstruction is a monumental building, showcasing the grandeur of the original temple. Situated in the ancient city of Ephesus, it was dedicated to Artemis, the goddess of the hunt.

Roman Agora

The Roman Agora in Athens was a marketplace and civic space, constructed during the Roman period. This open space contained a number of buildings, including the famous Tower of the Winds, a clock tower with intricate astronomical functions.

Tower of the Winds

The Tower of the Winds is a marble clocktower in the Roman Agora in Athens. It is notable for its intricate frieze, which represents the eight winds, and its function as a water clock. The tower still stands as a symbol of ancient Greek ingenuity in timekeeping.
